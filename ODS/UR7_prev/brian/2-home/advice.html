Your home - what to expect

You have £781.90 interest in your property.

Your interest is your share of after any secure debts (eg, your mortgage) have been paid. and allowing for the interest(s) of any joint owner(s).

How is interest calculated?
  • Equity = value of your property – mortgages & secured loans
  • Your interest = equity / the number of joint owners

What this may mean for you

Your interest in 110 High Street falls under £1,000 and you have dependents who live with you. This means that:

  • No immediate action will be taken against your home by the official receiver.
  • The value of your interest will be looked at again in 3 years time.
  • If in 3 years the value remains under £1,000 the official receiver won’t take any action your interest in 110 High Street will be returned to you at no cost and you will be able to keep your home.
  • You can buy your interest in 110 High Street back from the official receiver before the end of the 3 years. Usually, the money needed is paid for by a friend, relative or joint owner. Buying back your interest in [the property] means that your home can’t be sold to pay your debts..

What this may mean for you

Because your interest is between £1,000 and £10,000 and because you have dependants that live with you, it’s likely that no immediate action will be taken against your home.

Although this is normally the case, it will be up to the Official Receiver to look at your particular circumstances and make a final decision.

What will likely happen next:

  • In 3 years, the official receiver will check whether your interest in your home has changed.
  • As long as your interest is still less than £10,000 the official receiver will apply for a charge against your property. The charge will be for the value of your interest at that time.
  • The charge will remain against your property until you decide to sell it, much in the same way a mortgage does..
  • Interest will be added to the charge until your property is sold.
  • When you sell your property: 
    • The charge and any interest will be repaid (as far as possible)..
    • If your interest in the property is greater than the charge and any interest, you’ll receive the surplus.
    • If your interest in the property is less than the charge and any interest, you won’t be liable for the shortfall, and it will be written-off.

What you can do

  • You don't have to do anything, but you can buy your interest in 110 High Street back from the official receiver at any time over the next 3 years.
  • Buying back your interest in your home means that the official receiver won’t apply for a charge against your property, even if the value of your interest in the property increases.
