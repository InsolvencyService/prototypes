Your other assets

We need to know about certain items you own such as antiques, paintings, stocks and investments.

Telling us about an item doesn’t necessarily mean it will be taken away from you. Once your bankruptcy order is made, a full list of all your assets will be passed to a trustee. This is either an Insolvency Service official called the Official Receiver (OR) or a private Insolvency Practitioner.

Any decision about what will happen to your assets will be made by the trustee based on your personal circumstances.

Cash

What kinds of items to include

  • Cash not in a building society or bank
  • Positive account balances (eg if you have £500 in a building society, checking or bank account)

Household items of exceptional value

What kinds of items to include

  • Don’t include general household items that cost less than £1,500 (eg washing machine, oven, etc)
  • Do include items of significant value, for example items that you have insured individually on your contents insurance.

What kinds of items to include
