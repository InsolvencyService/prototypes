Your options:

  • Government option
  • Government Option
  • Debt management plan
  • Individual voluntary arrangement

Pros

  • Lasts one year
  • A government official (called an Official Receiver) manages the repayment of your debts
  • All creditors are bound by the arrangement
  • Unsecured creditors must deal with Official Receiver (not you)
  • Unsecured creditors can’t take legal action against you
  • Interest and charges are frozen
  • You pay back what you can afford
  • What you can’t repay is cleared

Cons

  • Your assets can be used to pay off your debts
  • Your extra income can also go towards paying off your debts - payments last for a maximum of 3 years
  • Stays on your credit report for six years
  • Your arrangement is entered on a public register for 15 months
  • If you own a business, it might be closed down
  • There are restrictions against being a company director and/or applying for credit - last for 1 year

Pros

  • Lasts one year
  • All qualifying debts written off after a year
  • You are free to keep your assets
  • A government official (Official Receiver) contacts your creditors for you
  • Creditors are bound by the arrangement
  • All interest and charges are frozen
  • Relatively inexpensive

Cons

  • You can only apply if your debts total less than £15,000
  • If you own your own home or have a mortgage you are unlikely to be eligible
  • The DRO will stay on your credit report for 6 years
  • Your arrangement is entered on a public register for 15 months
  • Certain changes in your circumstance could lead to the cancelling of the order
  • A DRO will impose some restrictions on your ability to obtain credit and act as a director of a company

Pros

  • Debt management company negotiates and manages the repayment of your debts.
  • Make one payment per month to the debt management company
  • Payment amount is based on what you can afford

Cons

  • You continue making payments until you’ve paid off all of your debts.
  • Creditors don’t have to agree to the plan.
  • Creditors can still contact you directly for repayment.
  • Creditors can still take legal action against you.
  • Your credit report is negatively affected until debts are paid off.
  • Interest and charges can still be added to your debts.
  • Your monthly repayments can be renegotiated at any time.

Pros

  • Typically lasts five years
  • A licensed person (called an Insolvency Practitioner) negotiates and manages the repayment of your debts.
  • Make one payment per month to the Insolvency Practitioner.
  • All creditors are bound by the arrangement.
  • Unsecured creditors must deal with Insolvency Practitioner (not you).
  • Unsecured creditors can’t take legal action against you.
  • Interest and charges are frozen.
  • You pay back what you can afford.
  • What you can’t repay is cleared.

Cons

  • Your assets can be used to pay off your debts
  • Your extra income can also go towards paying off your debts – payments last for a maximum of five years
  • If you miss payments your debt relief and protection from creditors will be lost
  • Stays on your credit report for 10 years
  • Your arrangement is entered on a public register for 63 months
  • You are restricted from applying for credit while the arrangement is in place
